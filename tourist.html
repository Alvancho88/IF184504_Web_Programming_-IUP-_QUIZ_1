   1. KRATON SURAKARTA HADININGRAT


   Keraton Surakarta Hadiningrat is the official Palace of the Surakarta Hadiningrat Sunanate which is located in Surakarta City.
   This palace has a fraction, namely the Yogyakarta Hadiningrat Palace from the Yogyakarta Sultanate.
   The Mataram dynasty was continued by 2 Kingdoms, namely the Surakarta Sunanate and the Yogyakarta Sultanate.
   The total area of ​​the Surakarta Palace is 147 hectares.
   Covering the entire area inside the Baluwarti Fort, Lor Square, Kidul Square, Gladak Gate, and the Surakarta Grand Mosque complex.
   Some of the palace complexes are museums that stored various gifts from European kings, replicas of palace heirlooms, and gamelan.


   2. FORT VASTENBURG


   Fort Vastenburg is an 18th-century Dutch fort located in Gladak, Surakarta, Indonesia.
   Fort Vastenburg is a square shaped fort with four bastions and three portals.
   It is surrounded by 6 meters high bearing wall and dry moat with a drawbridge.
   Fort Vastenburg was one of a series of defense network set up by the VOC.
   The main function of the fort was to house the colonial administrator and army.


   3. NATIONAL PRESS MUSEUM


   The National Press Monument is a monument and museum to the national Indonesian press.
   The complex consists of an old society building, used for the first meeting of the Indonesian Journalists Association
   The National Press Monument has a collection of over a million newspapers and magazines.


   4. SANGIRAN MUSEUM OF FOSSIL


   Sangiran Archaeological Museum is an archaeological museum located in Kalijambe, Sragen Regency, Central Java, Indonesia.
   The Sangiran Museum and its archaeological sites, apart from being an attractive tourist attraction,
   are also the most important and most comprehensive research arena on prehistoric life in Asia, and even the world.


   5. DANAR HADI BATIK MUSEUM


   House of Danar Hadi is an integrated heritage tourism complex about batik located in the city of Solo in Central Java.
   This museum stores a collection of batik cloth that reaches 10,000 pieces and is recognized by MURI as largest batik collection.
   This building was built at the end of the 19th century with a unique architectural style which is a combination of Javanese-European
