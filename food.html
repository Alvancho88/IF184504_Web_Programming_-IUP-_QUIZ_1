   Alright, Let's Start!!!

   1. NASI LIWET


   Nasi liwet is an Indonesian dish rice dish cooked in coconut milk, chicken broth and spices.
   Served alongside nasi liwet is opor ayam, telur pindang, tempeh and labu siam as the vegetable.
   In Solo, nasi liwet is usually eaten for breakfast, but also a popular choice for lunch or supper.


   2. SRABI SOLO


   Srabi is a typical snack from Solo made from coconut milk mixed with a little flour.
   Usually also given a sprinkling of pieces of banana, jackfruit or even meses and cheese if you like.
   The famous Srabi comes from the Notokusuman area, which is often shortened to Srabi Notosuman.


   3. TIMLO


   Timlo is a kind of soup with components of beancurd and egg or meat (chicken or beef) as the filling.
   Timlo has several variations. Timlo from the city of Solo is one that many people know.
   The most well known variant of Timlo is Timlo Sastro.


   4. TENGKLENG


   Tengkleng is a soup-like dish with goat bones as the main ingredient.
   The most known form of tengkleng is almost similar to goat curry, but the sauce is thinner.
   There is also tengkleng with a little gravy.


   5. SELAT SOLO


   Selat solo (Javanese for: "Solo salad") is a Javanese dish influenced by Western cuisine
   It consists of braised beef tenderloin served in thin watery sauce made from a mixture of garlic, vinegar,
   sweet soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, water, and spiced with nutmeg and black pepper.
   It is served with hard boiled eggs and vegetables such as string beans, potato, tomato, lettuce, cucumber,
   cauliflower and carrot, and topped with potato chips and some dash of mustard or mayonnaise on the side.
